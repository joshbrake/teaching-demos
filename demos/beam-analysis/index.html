Concepts

Support reactions are forces (and moments) that keep the beam in static equilibrium.

The shear force V(x) at any section equals the sum of vertical forces to the left of that section.

The bending moment M(x) equals the sum of moments of all forces to the left about that section.

Key relationship: dM/dx = V(x), so M is maximum where V = 0.

Sign Convention
+V: left part pushes up
+M: sagging (concave up)
−M: hogging (concave down)
ΣFy = 0   ΣM = 0
V(x) = ΣF left of x
M(x) = ΣM left of x
dV/dx = −w(x)   dM/dx = V(x)
